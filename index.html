On June 20, 2023, the British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP) conducted a new Skills Immigration draw. This latest BC PNP draw issued a total of 149 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) to eligible candidates present in the BC PNP pool.

This was the third Skills Immigration draw conducted by British Columbia in June.

Check eligibility to apply for BC PNP in 60 seconds

Latest BC PNP Draw: Who was invited?

Category 1: Targeted Draw (Tech)

  • Total number of invitations: 107
  • Streams invited: Skilled Workers & International Graduates (including EEBC option) and Entry-Level & Semi-Skilled
  • Minimum score: 90

Category 2: Targeted Draw (Childcare: Early childhood educators and assistants (NOC 42202))

  • Total number of invitations: 18
  • Streams invited: Skilled Workers and International Graduates (including EEBC option)
  • Minimum score: 60

Category 3: Targeted Draw (Healthcare)

  • Total number of invitations: 19
  • Streams invited: Skilled Workers and International Graduates (including EEBC option)
  • Minimum score: 60

Category 4: Targeted Draw (Other Priority Occupations (NOCs 31103, 32104))

  • Total number of invitations: 5
  • Streams invited: Skilled Workers and International Graduates (including EEBC option)
  • Minimum score: 60

BC PNP issues ITAs to eligible candidates on a weekly basis, with some exceptions. The frequency, size, and date of the next BC PNP draw - are determined by the needs of the BC PNP application inventory.

STEP 1: Determine your eligibility: Review the eligibility criteria for the BC PNP and ensure that you meet the requirements for the specific category you intend to apply under. Categories include Skilled Worker, Healthcare Professional, International Graduate, Entrepreneur, and more.

STEP 2: Create an online profile: If you are eligible, create an online profile through the BC PNP's online system, known as the Skills Immigration Registration System (SIRS). Provide accurate and up-to-date information about your education, work experience, language proficiency, and other relevant details.

STEP 3: Receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA): Regularly check the BC PNP draws and wait for an ITA. The BC PNP periodically invites candidates with the highest scores in the SIRS to apply for provincial nomination.

STEP 4: Submit a complete application: Once you receive an ITA, you will have a specific time frame to submit a complete application. This includes providing supporting documents such as educational transcripts, language test results, work experience letters, and proof of funds.

STEP 5: Wait for application processing: After submitting your application, the BC PNP will review it thoroughly. This process may take several months. Ensure that you promptly respond to any additional requests for information or documentation from the BC PNP.

STEP 6: Receive a provincial nomination: If your application is approved, you will receive a provincial nomination certificate from the BC PNP. This nomination allows you to apply for Canada PR to the federal government through the Express Entry system or other immigration pathways.
